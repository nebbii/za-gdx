**** Bell and Bouquet

    Location: Shrine of Strength entrance store
    Price: 10 Rupees (Bell), 20 Rupees (Bouquet)

These two items have no use whatsoever. If they do have one, it's so obscure and unimportant that they're basically useless.
**** Black Orb

    Location: Shrine of Illusion

This item is obtained after Zelda clears the geometric tile Patra room in the Shrine of Illusion, and can be placed in a receptacle in the colorful room to the east to open up a barred passageway.
**** Bone

    Location: Great Wimbich general store
    Price: 20 Rupees

When given to the shopkeeper's dog outside the general store, the Short Axe spell will appear near the bored man.
**** Candle

    Location: Forest of Ogham store, Great Wimbich town square store, Great Wimbich blacksmith, Seacoast Plain store
    Price: 100 Rupees
    Manual Description: "Use candles to lighten dark rooms. Each candle may be used only once."

Candles will lighten any dark room when used, and are a single-use item. Many stores around Tolemac sell a Candle, and will restock them once Zelda uses hers. There are only two dark rooms in the entire game, so they aren't particularly useful. I'll list them if you're curious:

    The last part of the cave maze in the Shrine of Earth
    The Stalfos maze in the Shrine of Illusion

**** Celestial Sign

    Location: Every Shrine

The Celestial Signs can be found at the very end of their respective Shrines. All seven are required to fight Gannon and beat the game. Whenever Zelda picks up one, she gains an extra heart container, as well as an increase in her defense and Wand strength.
**** Coal and Diamond

    Location: Plain of Hobel quarry (coal), Great Wimbich blacksmith (diamond)

This chunk of coal is given to Zelda by the Quarry Owner for free. It can be turned into a Diamond (also for free) by giving it to the Wimbich Smithy. Picking up the Diamond will completely fill Zelda's wallet with Rupees. It's best to do this after buying everything in the game so you can use it all for spellcasting.

The Diamond is also required to activate an easter egg in the Forest of Canvula. I won't spoil the result.
**** Compass

    Location: Every Shrine
    Manual Description: "When the shrines were first constructed, Gannon gave each shrine keeper a compass to the shrine he or she was to guard. These compasses have the power of teleportation, which is handy when you want to get to a shrine quickly."

Obtaining a Shrine's Compass will mark the location of the Celestial Sign room on the Shrine map. In addition, using a Compass in the overworld will warp Zelda to the outside entrance of the respective Shrine. This is very handy if you need to get somewhere that's halfway across Tolemac.

Here are the locations of every Compass:

    Shrine of Earth: Appears after defeating every Keese in the room south of the crossroads area.
    Shrine of Illusion: Appears after defeating the Wizzrobes in the tent room north of the vanishing bridge. Only the pair in the western half need to be killed to make the Compass appear.
    Shrine of Air: Appears after defeating every Patra in the room east of the first Vapora fight.
    Shrine of Destiny: Appears after defeating every Gibdo in the first Jack room.
    Shrine of Water: Found in the room after the North Kelpie fight. Watch out for the spike traps in the wall.
    Shrine of Strength: Given to Zelda by the Water Bearer south of the Shrine's entrance.
    Shrine of Fire: Appears after defeating the second and last Blacksmith in the Shrine.

**** Crystal Heart

    Location: Various places across Tolemac
    Manual Description: "A legend tells of four red crystal hearts hidden somewhere in the land of Tolemac. Each time Zelda finds and grabs one, her Life Meter increases by one heart."

These odd gems will add an extra heart to Zelda's life meter, much like what happens after she obtains a Celestial Sign. Four Crystal Hearts can be found in various places across Tolemac:

    In a cave east of the White Steed Lodge, within the Forest of Torian. Its entrance is surrounded by stones and is near the river. The Crystal Heart is hidden in a tree trunk maze inside the cave.
    On Mystery Island, accessible through a two Rupee boat tour offered by Faust the Boatman in the Barachno Marsh. Water spouts block the way to the Heart, which disappear upon collecting the Calm spell.
    In a cave north of South Port. It is located on the opposite side of a ravine, and requires Zelda to make a bridge appear by using a spread-fire spell to hit a crystal switch near the heart.
    Southeast of the Shrine of Fire entrance, on the opposite side of the river/lake near the Canvula village. It's hidden behind a tree.

**** DONE Empty Pitcher and Full Pitcher

    Location: Plain of Andor (empty pitcher), Andor River source (full pitcher)

Glebb will give Zelda an empty pitcher and ask her to fill it up at the river nearby. Using the pitcher at the river's source (the area with the stone Gannon head) will fill it up with water. After obtaining the full pitcher, Glebb will hand Zelda the Vial of Wind.
**** Fairy

    Location: Forest of Torian, South Gubasha Desert, Plain of Hobel

Technically, the fairies in this game aren't items, but... I have nowhere else to put them, so here we are. There are three fairies in this game, all of which live in pools hidden throughout Tolemac. Once Zelda touches a Fairy Fountain, all of her hearts will be restored.

Here are the locations of the three fairies:

    In the Forest of Torian, two screens north of the White Steed Lodge.
    In the South Gubasha Desert, two/three screens north of the town center.
    Near the Shrine of Strength's entrance, in a little alcove to the southeast.

**** Flute

    Location: Great Wimbich

Talking to Yvonne the musician will have her give Zelda the Flute. It can be used to defeat a snake southwest of Great Wimbich, which drops the Pyros spell upon death.
**** Golden Boots

    Location: Barachno Marsh

The Golden Boots can be obtained outside of Sirram Bew's house. They seem to have no use whatsoever.
**** Harp

    Location: Andor/Seacoast cave passage
    Manual Description: "The magical harp is known also to have the power of teleportation. It is rumored that, in shrines, it can be used to go back to the beginning of a shrine."

This musical instrument can be found in the cave connecting the Plain of Andor and the Seacoast Plain. Playing it in a Shrine will warp Zelda to its first room, allowing for a quick escape.
**** Keys

    Location: Shrine of Illusion
    Manual Description: "Use keys to open locked doors. To enter an open door, just walk up to it and go into the next room. Some doors lead nowhere; they are there to deceive you. When you have a key, you pass through and one key is subtracted from your inventory. Once a door is unlocked, it remains open for the rest of the game."

Don't let the description and 'double-digit' menu counter fool you: there are only two locked doors in the entire game, and both of them are in the Shrine of Illusion. Here are the find/unlock locations of each:

    The first key is in the red curtain room with the three doors, and appears after killing the lone Vire. It unlocks the door in the Pasquinade taunt room.
    The second key is in the first Molluska room, and appears after killing said enemies. It unlocks a door in the same room.

**** Knife

    Location: Shrine of Illusion

The Knife appears after defeating both Goriyas in the tent dead-end room. It is used to cut open a exit hole in the wall in the very same room. It will need to be used every time Zelda enters the dead-end room, as the hole she creates will seal up after leaving.
**** Ladder

    Location: Forest of Ogham

This ladder can be found leaning on a tree two screens south of the Ogham merchant. It is used to bridge a chasm in the Shrine of Earth.
**** Life Heart

    Location: Everywhere

Dropped by regular enemies, along with Rupees. They heal one of Zelda's hearts, as you'd expect.
**** Life Potion

    Location: Great Wimbich town square store, Plain of Donora store, Seacoast Plain store, Forest of Canvula store
    Price: 200 Rupees
    Manual Description: "This potion restores Zelda to full life."

This potion restores all of Zelda's hearts. It's as simple as that. The Life Potion can be used even if Zelda is at full health, so be wary of that.

Stores will restock their Life Potions upon the item's use, much like the Candle.
**** Magic Shield

    Location: Forest of Ogham store
    Price: 500 Rupees
    Manual Description: "This shield has magical abilities to protect Zelda from enemy weapon attacks. Because it uses magic, Zelda must concentrate to make it work."

This shield blocks all non-boss projectiles from every side if Zelda stands still. The shield does not have to be equipped to do this. You're rather unlikely to notice this, however, as you're probably moving around a bunch when fighting a group of projectile enemies.
**** Plank

    Location: Shrine of Water

The Plank can be found in the screen after the second wall spike room, and is used to bridge a gap in the broken bridge nearby.
**** Raft

    Location: Wimbich Pond
    Manual Description: "Ride a raft across water. If a raft is available, it appears in the location where it can be used, or it can be selected from the inventory screen. Walk onto the raft and it starts to move. You can walk around on the raft, but you can't get off until it stops at its destination."

You can find the Raft laying on the edge of the pond near Great Wimbich, directly to the south of the town center. It is needed to cross the Whirlpool in the Shrine of Water. (The raft in South Port that takes you to Sea Island will appear even if you do not have the item.)
**** Red Boots

    Location: Shrine of Earth

These snazzy boots can be found near the end of the cave maze in the Shrine of Earth. Once obtained, Zelda can cross the Andor River, and thus access the rest of Tolemac. The boots do not have to be equipped to do this.
**** Red Ribbon

    Location: Shrine of Strength entrance store
    Price: 50 Rupees

Unlike the Bell and Bouquet, the Red Ribbon actually has a use in the Shrine. Once it's bought, the Red Knight miniboss will appear in the Jousting Field, as the ribbon was stolen from him many ages ago.
**** Rug

    Location: Shrine of Air

This blue rug is dropped by the second Vapora in the Shrine. When equipped, the Rug makes Zelda slide around less on slippery surfaces. However, she can't attack with her wand while doing so, making the Rug a bit of a hassle to use.
**** Rupees

    Location: Everywhere
    Manual Description: "Collect rupees by defeating monsters. When you have at least one rupee, it appears in your inventory. If you wish to buy an item, stand close to it so the price flashes. Select the rupee from your inventory screen. If you have enough rupees to purchase the item, click Button One and it appears in your inventory. The cost in rupees will be subtracted from your total. Also, some creatures may demand that you give them money to pass through a gate or take a boat ride."

Rupees act a bit differently than what you'd expect from a regular Zelda game. For starters, there are only two types in the entire game; blue Rupees (5) and yellow Rupees (10). Rupees can be used to buy items, but only when Zelda has selected them in her inventory like an item. They are also required to power spells. Zelda can hold up to 999 Rupees.

Apart from defeating enemies, Zelda can acquire a lot of Rupees on four separate occasions:

    Defeating Llort in the Shrine of Earth (250 Rupees)
    Talking to Lonlyn in Verna (25 Rupees)
    Hitting the bears in the Shrine of Strength archery minigame (25, 50, and 100 Rupees in that order)
    Picking up the Diamond (999 Rupees)

The archery minigame can be reset by exiting and entering the Shrine, allowing for infinite Rupees.
**** Ticket

    Location: Shrine of Strength ticket booths
    Price: 20 Rupees each

These tickets allow Zelda to enter the Shrine of Strength's fields and challenge Ursore's three Knights. They can be bought from the vendors stationed at the fields' entrances. There are two to collect:

    The "ADMIT" ticket lets Zelda enter the Jousting Field and fight the Red Knight.
    The patterned ticket allows Zelda to visit the Field of the Broadsword and attack the Blue and Green Knights.

**** Underworld Map

    Location: Every Shrine

Works just like any Zelda game - they allow you to see the full layout of a Shrine. Here are the locations of every Underworld Map:

    Shrine of Earth: Appears after defeating all of the Tektites in the crossroads area.
    Shrine of Illusion: Appears after defeating the lone Vire in the mirror and red curtains room.
    Shrine of Air: Appears after defeating every Tornado in the entrance area.
    Shrine of Destiny: Surprise! This map can't be found inside the Shrine itself. Instead, it's in the Barachno Marsh, given to Zelda by Yelena, Link's former guide.
    Shrine of Water: Appears after defeating the Zols in the second spike wall area.
    Shrine of Strength: Appears after pushing the left block to the right in the block puzzle east of the entrance area.
    Shrine of Fire: Appears after defeating the Crockarocks in the area after the second Blacksmith screen.

**** Vial of Wind

    Location: Plain of Andor
    Manual Description: "Creates wind to move the rafts when there is no breeze."

Zelda can get this vial after handing Glebb the filled pitcher. It can be used with the South Port raft to access Sea Island and the Shrine of Water.

    The blue points are the damage points. If one of those points touches zelda, she takes damage."

        Red: Ogham ambush cave, Shrine of Earth
        Blue: Meadow of Skotness, South Gubasha Desert, Ubato Hills
    Health:
        Red: 2
        Blue: 3 (0-2 CS), 2 (3-6 CS)
    Behavior Type: Aggressive
    Damage:
        Red: 0.5 (0-2 CS), Negligible (3-6 CS)
        Blue: 1 (0-2 CS), 0.5 (3-6 CS)
    Weakness(es): All
    Drops: Nothing, Life Heart, 5 Rupees, 10 Rupees, Shrine of Earth map (one screen only)
    Knockback Distance: Large
    Technical Export (enemy.tektite):
        [health=60, damage=51, defense=5] on cells j16, k15, k16, l16, m14, m15, r5, r8, v10, v12, v9, x9
        [health=60, damage=40, defense=0] on cells q10, s101a, s103, s104, s116

Little spiders that love to crawl around in Tolemac's caverns, with the season bringing them (and Leevers) out onto the land's fields. You'll encounter two variants; a red Tektite only seen near and inside the Shrine of Earth, and a tougher blue Tektite encountered in the main overworld. Killing every blue Tektite at a crossroads in the Shrine of Earth will gift you the dungeon's Compass.

*** Tornado
    Location: Shrine of Air
    Health: 4
    Behavior Type: Aggressive
    Damage: 1 (2-4 CS), 0.5 (5-6 CS)
    Weakness(es): Calm
    Drops: Nothing, Life Heart, 5 Rupees, 10 Rupees, Shrine of Air map (one screen only)
    Knockback Distance: Large
    Technical Export (enemy.tornado):
        [health=280, damage=52, defense=32, weakness=Calm, bonusDamage=72]

These living whirlwinds protect the Shrine of Air's opening rooms, and can only be dispersed with four blasts from the Calm spell. Fortunately, there are only two groups of them, and destroying the first nets you the Shrine of Air's map.

*** Tumblebot
    Location: Plain of Andor, Plain of Donora
    Health: 2
    Behavior Type: Aggressive
    Damage: 0.5 (0 CS), Negligible (3 CS)
    Weakness(es): All
    Drops: Nothing, Life Heart, 5 Rupees, 10 Rupees
    Knockback Distance: Large
    Technical Export (enemy.tumbleSkull):
        [health=60, damage=57, defense=10]

Living blue boulders that roll around in Tolemac's two plains. They're very weak even at the start of the game, and their rather large clusters can result in easy Rupee and heart farming.

*** Vire
    Location: Shrine of Illusion
    Health: 4 (1-2 CS), 3 (3-6 CS)
    Behavior Type: Aggressive
    Damage: 1 (1-3 CS), 0.5 (4-6 CS)
    Weakness(es): All
    Drops: Key (southern), Shrine of Illusion map (northern), nothing (after their items are collected)
    Knockback Distance: Large
    Technical Export (enemy.vire):
        [health=60, damage=51, defense=15]

These burly bats guard two rooms in the southwestern corner of the Shrine of Illusion. Interestingly, both of them drop a unique item; the Vire in the southern room has a Key, while the one in the northern room drops the Shrine's map. Once their items are collected, they don't drop anything, not even the usual Rupee/heart.

*** Volta
    Location: Shrine of Air
    Health: 3 (2-4 CS), 2 (5-6 CS)
    Behavior Type: Aggressive
    Damage: 1 (2-4 CS), 0.5 (5-6 CS)
    Weakness(es): All
    Drops: Nothing, Life Heart, 5 Rupees, 10 Rupees
    Knockback Distance: Large
    Technical Export (enemy.volta):
        [health=60, damage=52, defense=10]

Some of Aviana's "feathered friends" which help guard the Shrine of Air. They're actually quite featherless in spite of the Shrine Queen's description. Apart from this small discrepancy, they're your usual mid-tier Shrine enemy.

*** Wallmaster
    Location: Shrine of Illusion
    Health: Immortal
    Behavior Type: Fixed
    Damage: Sending Zelda back to the Shrine's first room
    Weakness(es): None
    Drops: N/A
    Knockback Distance: N/A
    Technical Export (enemy.wallmaster):
        [health=0, damage=0, defense=0]

Large blue hands that try to grab Zelda; if successful, they send her to the first room of a Shrine. Luckily for you, they're quite slow, stay in one area and never chase the Princess. and only appear in a single room in the Shrine of Illusion.

*** Water Spout
    Location: Lake Boruna
    Health: Immortal
    Behavior Type: Fixed
    Damage: Blocking Faust the Boatman's canoe
    Weakness(es): Calm
    Drops: N/A
    Knockback Distance: N/A

These large water spouts form on the surface of Lake Boruna, located in the Forest of Albon and home to Mystery Island. If Zelda doesn't have the Calm spell, they'll block Faust the Boatman's canoe from arriving at Mystery Island, sending it back to the dock near his hut. After the spell is obtained, the spouts automatically disappear.

*** Wizzrobe
    Location: Shrine of Illusion, Shrine of Destiny
    Health: 4
    Behavior Type: Aggressive
    Damage:
        Melee: 1 (1 CS), 0.5 (2-4 CS), Negligible (5-6 CS)
        Projectile: 1 (1-3 CS), 0.5 (4-6 CS)
    Weakness(es): Dagger
    Drops: Nothing, Life Heart, 5 Rupees, 10 Rupees, Shrine of Illusion compass (one screen only)
    Knockback Distance: Large
    Technical Export (enemy.wizzrobe):
        [health=280, damage=45, defense=35, weakness=Dagger, bonusDamage=70]

Also known as "Great Magic Users", these robed runemakers guard places dedicated to abstract concepts, such as fate and illusions. Once using daggers as their means of attack, Wizzrobes have now switched to a spell similar to your Noise one. Perhaps you can use the former spell to vanquish them? The first group of Wizzrobes in the Shrine of Illusion drop the dungeon's Compass upon death.

*** Zol
    Location: Shrine of Water
    Health: 8 (4 CS), 6 (5 CS), 5 (6 CS)
    Behavior Type: Aggressive
    Damage: 1 (4 CS), 0.5 (5-6 CS)
    Weakness(es): All
    Drops: Nothing, Life Heart, 5 Rupees, 10 Rupees, Shrine of Water map (one screen only)
    Knockback Distance: Large
    Technical Export (enemy.zol):
        [health=60, damage=54, defense=30]

A big red blob that can be see in the first half of the Shrine of Water. They don't split into smaller blobs upon their defeat... probably a good thing. Destroying the first group of Zols makes the Shrine's map appear.

*** Zola (River)
    Location: Forest of Torian
    Health: 6 (0 CS), 5 (1-2 CS), 4 (3-4 CS), 3 (5-6 CS)
    Behavior Type: Stationary
    Damage:
        Melee: 1 (0-3 CS), 0.5 (4-6 CS)
        Projectile: 1 (0-5 CS), 0.5 (6 CS)
    Weakness(es): All
    Drops: Nothing, Life Heart, 5 Rupees, 10 Rupees
    Knockback Distance: None

A green-tinted Zola that resides in Skotness River and spits spiked balls at Zelda. Unlike their main series counterparts, these Zola (and their cousins) prefer to always stay above water.

*** Zola (Sea)
    Location: North Seacoast Plain, South Seacoast Plain
    Health: Immortal (0 CS), 30 (1 CS), 15 (2 CS), 10 (3 CS), 8 (4 CS), 6 (5 CS), 5 (6 CS)
    Behavior Type: Stationary
    Damage:
        Melee: 1.5 (0-4 CS), 1 (5-6 CS)
        Projectile: 1.5 (0-3 CS), 1 (4-6 CS)
    Weakness(es): All
    Drops: Nothing
    Knockback Distance: None
    Technical Export (possible match: enemy.seaMonster):
        [health=60, damage=58, defense=30]

A blue-tined Zola that lives in the ocean next to the Seacoast Plain. They're much tougher than their two cousins, having the same HP issue as the Sea Eagle and dealing an extra half-heart of damage. Best to dodge their projectiles and ignore them.

*** Zola (Swamp)
    Location: Barachno Marsh, Swamp of Vendoss
    Health: 6 (0 CS), 5 (1-2 CS), 4 (3-4 CS), 3 (5-6 CS)
    Behavior Type: Stationary
    Damage:
        Melee: 1 (0-1 CS), 0.5 (2-6 CS)
        Projectile: 1 (0-5 CS), 0.5 (6 CS)
    Weakness(es): All
    Drops: Nothing, Life Heart, 5 Rupees, 10 Rupees
    Knockback Distance: None
    Technical Export:
        enemy.swampZola.green:
            [health=60, damage=50, defense=20]
        enemy.swampZola.blue:
            [health=60, damage=62, defense=30]

These Zola are very similar to their river-dwelling counterparts, apart from two small things. First, they're only found in the Forest of Albon, stuck in its murky swamp waters. Secondly, you can buy repellant spray from the nearby Barachno Marsh to make them disappear for good.
